# References and Resources

## Overview

This section provides curated resources for deepening your knowledge in Physical AI, prompt engineering, and robotics.

---

## Foundational Books

### AI and Machine Learning

**"Deep Learning" by Goodfellow, Bengio, and Courville**
- 800+ page comprehensive textbook
- Mathematical foundations for deep learning
- Industry standard reference
- Recommended background: Linear algebra, calculus

**"Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction" by Sutton and Barto**
- Authoritative RL textbook
- Algorithms and theory
- Includes code examples
- Essential for robot learning

**"Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning" by Bishop**
- Probabilistic approaches
- Graphical models, Bayesian methods
- Classical ML foundations
- Dense but thorough

### Robotics

**"Introduction to Robotics" by John J. Craig**
- Standard robotics textbook
- Kinematics, dynamics, control
- Mathematical rigor
- Industrial robotics focus

**"Robotics: Modelling, Planning and Control" by Siciliano et al.**
- Comprehensive modern textbook
- Mobile robots and manipulators
- 600+ pages
- Mathematical and practical coverage

**"A Gentle Introduction to ROS" by Jason O'Kane**
- ROS tutorial specifically designed for beginners
- Hands-on exercises
- Free online version available
- Perfect starting point

### Computer Vision

**"Computer Vision: Algorithms and Applications" by Richard Szeliski**
- Comprehensive CV reference
- From fundamentals to deep learning
- 832 pages
- Free draft online

## Research Papers

### Transformative AI Papers

**"Attention is All You Need" (Vaswani et al., 2017)**
- Introduces Transformer architecture
- Foundation for modern LLMs
- ~30,000 citations
- Readable for understanding modern AI

**"ImageNet-Trained CNNs are Biased Towards Texture" (Geirhos et al., 2018)**
- Reveals CNN behavior surprising to ML engineers
- Important for understanding perception limitations
- Real implications for robotics

**"Hallucination in Neural-Network-based Q-Learning" (Thakur et al., 2022)**
- Addresses key limitation of RL in robotics
- How agents learn incorrect behaviors
- Solutions for safe learning

### Robotics Papers

**"Recent Advances in Robot Motion Planning" (Otte et al., 2016)**
- Survey of modern path planning
- RRT, PRM, and variants
- Real-world applications

**"A Review of Collaborative Robots" (Djuric et al., 2016)**
- Comprehensive cobot overview
- Safety standards review
- Industry trends

## Online Courses

### Comprehensive

**"CS231n: Convolutional Neural Networks for Visual Recognition"** (Stanford)
- Free online course from Stanford
- Computer vision and CNNs
- Video lectures available
- Assignments teach TensorFlow/PyTorch

**"CS50's Introduction to AI"** (Harvard)
- Broad AI survey course
- Covers ML, NLP, computer vision, robotics
- Free on edX
- Beginner-friendly

### Deep Learning

**"Deep Learning Specialization"** (Coursera)
- 5-course sequence by Andrew Ng
- Hands-on TensorFlow training
- Certificates available
- Practical and theoretical

**"Practical Deep Learning for Coders"** (Fast.ai)
- Top-down approach (application first)
- Free with optional paid version
- PyTorch-based
- Project-oriented

### Robotics

**"Modern Robotics Specialization"** (Coursera)
- 6-course sequence by Kevin Lynch (Northwestern)
- Kinematics, dynamics, control, planning
- MATLAB/Python assignments
- Industry-standard curriculum

**"Introduction to Robotics"** (MIT OpenCourseWare)
- Free MIT robotics course materials
- Lectures and problem sets
- Advanced undergraduate level
- Comprehensive content

### Prompt Engineering

**"ChatGPT Prompt Engineering for Developers"** (DeepLearning.AI)
- Free short course by Andrew Ng and Isa Fulton
- Practical prompt techniques
- OpenAI API examples
- Production best practices

## Conferences and Events

### Research Conferences

**ICRA** (International Conference on Robotics and Automation)
- Premier robotics conference
- Thousands of papers annually
- Accepted papers available online

**IROS** (International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems)
- Major robotics conference
- Autonomous systems focus
- Papers on arxiv before conference

**NeurIPS** (Neural Information Processing Systems)
- Top ML conference
- Cutting-edge research
- Papers, videos, workshops

**ICCV** (International Conference on Computer Vision)
- Computer vision research
- State-of-art techniques
- Free paper access

### Community Events

**ROS World**
- Annual ROS community conference
- Tutorials, talks, networking
- Free online option usually available

**ROSCON** (ROS Conference)
- Community-oriented ROS conference
- Workshops and tutorials
- Smaller than ROS World, more intimate

- 330K images, 80 object categories
- Object detection, segmentation
- Industry standard

**ImageNet**
- Millions of labeled images
- 1000 object categories
- Used for pretraining models

**RoboNet**
- Robot-generated data
- Thousands of hours video

## Summary of Learning Path

1. **Start**: Foundational course (CS50 or Fast.ai)
2. **Deepen**: Specialize by interest
   - Deep learning: Deep Learning Specialization
   - Robotics: Modern Robotics or MIT course
   - Computer vision: Stanford CS231n
3. **Practice**: Build projects with tutorials
4. **Research**: Read papers, follow conferences
5. **Contribute**: Share knowledge, publish findings

Resources are abundant. Choose one path, go deep, expand as needed.
